Impacts of COVID-19

Where has Covid-19 had the Most Impact?

COVID-19 is a contagious disease that has spread world wide since it was first identified in Decemeber 2019. It's had an impact on every country, leading to lockdowns, restictions, deaths, effects on the economy, etc. Countries in Europe, North and South America have incurred the highest death tolls relative to their population, with as high as 6,409 deaths per million in Peru.

Is there a Correlation with Vaccination Rates?

COVID-19 Vaccinations are extremely important in combatting this disease, however, as you can see from the chart on the left they aren't the only factor to consider. Africa has the lowest vaccination rate of all continents, yet has been the least impacted by COVID-19. This is due to the quick responses taken from their governments in order to slow the spread of the virus, the support from the public to adhere with COVID-19 measures, their young population, having a median age of 19, as well as a favourable climate with a high temperature and humidity

Is COVID-19 Decreasing Worldwide?

Both the severity and amount of cases has reduced since its peak in the beginning of 2022. Vaccination rates in Australia have increased drastically since they were first available on the 22nd of February 2021. By the start of February 2022, 78.8% of the population was fully vaccinated, above you can see that following the peak in January 2022, the total cases and deaths has been trending downwards. As of the 12th of October 2022, 90.6% of Australians have been fully vaccinated.
